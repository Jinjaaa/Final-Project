The course section offers a diverse array of academic disciplines, ranging from architecture and agriculture to business and health sciences. Each course is meticulously curated to provide comprehensive knowledge and skill development opportunities for students seeking to explore their interests and pursue their academic and professional goals.

ARCHITECTURE

Architecture is a multifaceted discipline that encompasses the art, science, and practice of designing and constructing buildings and other physical structures. In an introductory course, students delve into fundamental concepts such as spatial organization, structural principles, historical context, and design theory.


Courses below Architecture and Design

  • Bachelor of Science in Architecture
  • Bachelor in Landscape Architecture
  • Bachelor of Science in Interior Design

Some examples of subjects that you may take while enrolled in this program include:

  • Strength of Materials
  • Theory of Structures
  • Steel and Timber Design
  • Architectural Structures
  • Surveying
  • Statics of Rigid Bodies
  • Architectural Visual Communication
  • Architectural Design
  • Architectural Interiors
  • Building Technology

CAREERS

Qualified graduates of BS Architecture can find employment in various architecture-related fields such as:

  • Facility Planning
  • Construction Management
  • Building Administration and Maintenance
  • Real Estate Development

LICENSURE EXAMINATION

Passing the Licensure Examination for Architects is one of the requirements in seeking employment in the architectural industry. Please visit the official website of the Professional Regulatory Commission (PRC) for more information.
