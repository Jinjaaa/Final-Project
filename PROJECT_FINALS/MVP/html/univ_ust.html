FACILITIES

UST Building

The University of Santo Tomas (UST) is renowned for its stunning campus architecture, characterized by a harmonious blend of Spanish colonial and neoclassical styles. The Main Building, with its iconic facade and grand central staircase, stands as a symbol of UST's rich heritage and academic tradition. As the oldest existing university building in Asia, it serves as the centerpiece of the campus and houses administrative offices, classrooms, and historic halls that echo with centuries of learning and scholarship.

Library

UST's Miguel de Benavides Library is a treasure trove of knowledge and learning, housing a vast collection of books, manuscripts, and archival materials. With its expansive reading rooms, digital resources, and specialized collections, the library provides students and scholars with access to a wealth of information across various disciplines. As a hub for intellectual inquiry and research, the library plays a central role in UST's academic community, fostering a culture of scholarship and lifelong learning among its patrons.

Gym

UST's Sports Complex offers students a modern and well-equipped facility for physical fitness and sports activities. From state-of-the-art fitness centers to indoor courts and swimming pools, the gym provides students with ample opportunities to stay active and healthy. Additionally, UST's Sports Complex hosts intramural leagues, intercollegiate competitions, and recreational programs that promote teamwork, sportsmanship, and holistic development among students. With its vibrant sports culture and commitment to excellence, UST's gymnasium embodies the university's motto of "Virtus et Scientia" – Virtue and Knowledge.
